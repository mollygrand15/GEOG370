Description of Assignment:

In order to change the projection model used in each display, I changed the projection using the icon in the bottom right corner in QGIS, and used the Indicatrix Matrix to view how each projection distorted the maps.

WGS84 Projection

WGS84 uses a conformal projection, and while it preserves direction and the shape of geographic regions, it distorts distance and area. From the circles generated from the Indicatrix Matrix, we can see that this projection especially distorts the distance and area near the poles.

Aitoff Projection

The Aitoff projection distorts shape, area, distance, and direction. While there appears to be less stretching at the center of the map, this distortion can be seen by the shapes of the circles that are towards the outer edges of the map.

Pseudo Mercator

The Pseudo Mercator projection preserves the shape of countries but distorts the relative size of the area of landmasses. Since this projection especially exaggerates areas near the north and south pole, we can see that Greenland appears to be the same size as Africa.

Winkel Sphere

It appears that the Winkel Sphere projection generally distorts the shapes of regions that are near the east and west edges of the map. The geographic regions in the center of the map appear to maintain their area and shape relatively well.

World Cylindrical Equal Area

The World Cylindrical Equal Area projection is not distorted along the equator, however distortion does increase towards the poles. This projection extremely distorts the shape of landmasses, and this can be seen by the stretched out circles at the top and bottom of the map.

World Equidistant Conic

The World Equidistant Conic projection preserves distances along all meridians and two standard parallels. However, the shape, area, distance, and direction are all generally distorted throughout the entire map. The distance at the bottom of the image appears to suffer the most from distortion.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant

The North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant Projection preserves both distance and direction. Shape and area appear to be distorted throughout the entire map and this distortion appears to incease outward from the center point.

Winkel Tripel

While the Winkel Tripel projection generally distorts the shape, area, distance, and direction of geographic regions, it maintains a nice balance between size and shape projection throughout the map. The distortion of the shape of geographic areas can especially be seen in regions like Norway and Iceland.

World Loximuthal

In the World Loximuthal projection, it appears that the map becomes more distorted further away from the center. It also appears that the overall shape of the world becomes distorted, and looks like a pumpkin, where the sides are outstretched.
